Part VII. Verifying the Analytical Method's Accuracy

Investigation 33: Spike-Recovery Studies

Although the results of Investigation 31 and Investigation 32 provide confidence in our analytical method, we have not yet established that the concentrations we report represent accurately the actual concentrations of our analytes in Danshen. The possibility of thermal degradation during the microwave extraction, for example, is a potential concern not addressed in Investigation 32.

Establishing our method's accuracy is complicated because we do not know, a priori, the actual concentrations of the hydrophilic compounds and the lipophilic compounds in any particular sample of Danshen; indeed, their concentrations certainly vary from plant-to-plant and from field-to-field, particularly plants and fields in different geographic regions, and likely are affected by methods of cultivation.
